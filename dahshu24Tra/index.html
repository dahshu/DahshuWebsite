Venue Information

Airport Information and Ground Transportation

For those flying in for the DahShu Data Science Symposium, several airports serve the East Lansing area, offering a range of options depending on your travel preferences and origins.

Detroit Metropolitan Wayne County Airport (DTW) Google Map, located in Romulus, MI, is a major international airport providing extensive flight options. For convenience, the Michigan Flyer offers reliable and comfortable bus service from DTW to East Lansing. For schedules and bookings, please visit Michigan Flyer.

Gerald R. Ford International Airport (GRR) Google Map in Grand Rapids, MI, and Capital Region International Airport (LAN) Google Map in Lansing, MI, are closer alternatives that might offer a more direct route to East Lansing for some travelers.

We recommend reviewing all options to determine the most convenient and cost-effective route for your journey to the symposium. Each airport offers different advantages in terms of travel time to East Lansing, flight availability, and transportation options. Please ensure to plan your ground transportation in advance to ensure a smooth continuation of your journey to East Lansing.

Attractions or Things to do

Day Sightseeing

For those looking to extend their adventure beyond the Michigan State University campus, a plethora of day-trip destinations await. Venture into Downtown Lansing, a short drive away, to immerse yourself in a vibrant mix of shopping, dining, and cultural experiences, including the historic Michigan State Capitol with its stunning architecture and public tours. Old Town Lansing offers a unique blend of boutique shops, galleries, cafes, and restaurants, set within a lively community atmosphere, perfect for experiencing local culture and art. The Michigan History Museum, located in Lansing, provides an insightful journey through the state's rich history and heritage. For nature and adventure enthusiasts, Grand Ledge, about a 30-minute drive from campus, boasts spectacular rock formations and a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, rock climbing, and river kayaking. These destinations offer a fulfilling and memorable day out for conference attendees, showcasing the diverse attractions Michigan has to offer.
